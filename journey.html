My Journey

I was born in Odesa, Ukraine, growing up my life spun around the basketball court. Following in my father's footsteps, I developed a genuine love for this game.

At 14, I managed to get a scholarship in California to play there, before the COVID-19 eventually transferred me to Wichita, Kansas in 2020. It was there that I took my first introduction to programming class.

While taking this class, I began writing small Python scripts to help myself with basic algebra homework. This helped me to save some free time on it and keep up with intensee basketball season and 2 trainings per day. Seeing and learning how computer could handle several tasks more efficiently it sparked my interest in exploring how technology and later machine learning and AI can help people work faster or study better.

After graduation in 2023, I gained my first real-world experience as a team intern for We Are D3, a professional basketball team competing in the $1 million TBT tournament.

Seeing how things run behind the scenes allowed me to gain experience in team management and further explore more how data is used everywhere in sports by being the "secret" behind the strategy, similar to what is shown in the Moneyball (2011) movie with Brad Pitt and Johan Hill.

After that summer, I went to play college basketball at GCC in Michigan for NJCAA Region 13 during the 2023/24 season. I then chose to study Computer Programming in Applied Business to dive deeper into the areas that interested me.

While that year wasn’t the best for basketball, it was definitely a great year for exploring pick of AI and even getting my first introduction to Web3.

After finishing my freshman year of college, I returned to We Are D3 in the summer of 2024. I traveled with the team to Dayton, Ohio, to witness the team's first tournament win in six years.

→ Spoiler: Watch the first ever win emotions

A summer in Ohio that year, along with living and traveling around New England with my family, made me realize that I wanted to learn more about the science behind the rapidly changing world of AI and data-driven decisions - the kind that can help people win or improve outcomes.

That’s why I made the hard decision to end my basketball career in the states, where I had spent nearly seven years of my life, and focus on my new goal. I moved to Aberdeen to pursue a degree in Data Science with Business Management at Robert Gordon University.

And yeah, I still play and enjoy basketball in my free time here in Scotland, but I’m also exploring an idea I’ve had since my sophomore year of high school - that Machine Learning and AI can be good for people, as long as we make the right decisions in how we want to use it to improve things around us.
